Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your Himalayan adventure.

Booking & Payments

How far in advance should I book my trek?
We recommend booking at least 4–8 weeks in advance, especially for peak season (March–May, September–November). Popular routes like EBC can fill up months early. Last-minute treks are sometimes available — contact us directly.
What payment methods do you accept?
We accept bank transfers, credit/debit cards, and PayPal. A 30% deposit confirms your booking, with the balance due 30 days before departure.
What is your cancellation policy?
45+ days: full refund minus 5% fee. 30–44 days: 50% refund. Under 30 days: non-refundable. We strongly recommend travel insurance that covers cancellation.

Health & Safety

Do I need travel insurance?
Yes — travel insurance with high-altitude helicopter evacuation cover is mandatory for all treks. We require proof of coverage of at least $100,000 in medical evacuation.
How fit do I need to be for Everest Base Camp?
You should be comfortable hiking 5–7 hours per day carrying a 5–8kg daypack. Regular aerobic exercise for 2–3 months before departure is strongly recommended.

Logistics & Permits

Are permits included in the trek price?
Yes. All national park entry fees, TIMS cards, and regional permits are included. Our team handles everything before your arrival.
What is the best time of year to trek in Nepal?
The two main seasons are Spring (March–May) and Autumn (September–November). Both offer stable weather and excellent visibility. Monsoon (June–August) is generally not recommended for high-altitude routes.
