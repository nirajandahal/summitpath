Best Time of Year to Trek Everest Base Camp

Pemba Sherpa
Senior Mountain Guide · May 14, 2025 · 6 min read

After guiding over 200 trekkers to Everest Base Camp across 15 seasons, I'm often asked one question before anything else: when should I go? The answer matters more than most people realise. Choose the wrong month and you might walk 130km in cloud, never once seeing the mountain you came for. Choose the right window and you'll have days so clear you can watch the plume streaming off Everest's summit from your teahouse window.

The short answer: March to May (Spring) and late September to November (Autumn). Everything else is a compromise. Here's why — and how to choose between them.

Spring: March to May

Spring is the most popular trekking season, and for good reason. The rhododendron forests below Namche burst into red and pink bloom from mid-March, the days grow longer, and temperatures at altitude are rising rather than falling. Most importantly, the pre-monsoon weather produces reliably clear mornings — which is when you'll be trekking.

April is the sweet spot. Days are warm enough at lower elevations to be comfortable in a single layer, and the snowpack from winter has mostly consolidated, making trails well-defined. It's also Everest climbing season — if you time it right you'll see expedition teams and a parade of yaks carrying equipment toward Base Camp, which adds an atmosphere nothing else can replicate.

"On a clear April morning at Kala Patthar, the summit of Everest catches the first light a full twenty minutes before the valley below. I've watched grown men and women go completely silent. That moment never gets old."

The downside of Spring is that trails are busier, teahouses fill up, and the last two weeks of May see afternoon clouds building earlier as the monsoon approaches. If you're trekking in late May, start each day at first light.

Autumn: Late September to November

Autumn is my personal preference. The monsoon scrubs the atmosphere clean of dust and haze, so visibility in October is often sharper than anything Spring can offer. The valleys are lush and green, waterfalls are still running, and the post-monsoon light has a quality that photographers obsess over.

October is peak season — trails are busier than ever and advance booking is essential. For a quieter experience, aim for early October (trails are slightly muddy from late monsoon but manageable) or push into November when most trekkers have gone home. November is colder — expect -15°C nights at Gorak Shep — but the trails are peaceful and the sky almost comically blue.

Altitude tip: Whatever month you choose, never skip the acclimatisation day in Namche Bazaar. The two nights at 3,440m are non-negotiable — they are the single most effective thing you can do to prevent altitude sickness higher on the trail.

What About Winter and Monsoon?

December to February is cold but not impossible. Trails are quiet, teahouse prices drop, and on clear days the views are exceptional. The main risk is heavy snowfall closing the Cho La and Kongma La passes, and Lukla flights are frequently cancelled. For experienced cold-weather trekkers this is a genuinely rewarding option — just build in buffer days at either end for weather delays.

June to August — monsoon season — is not recommended for the EBC route. Rain is near-daily, leeches are active below 3,000m, trails become dangerously slippery, and cloud obscures views for days at a time. Some trekkers do go during monsoon, but they do so with very low expectations and appropriate waterproofing.

Month-by-Month at a Glance

  • January–February: Cold, quiet, risky for snow. For experienced trekkers only.
  • March: Warming up, rhododendrons blooming, excellent choice.
  • April: Best overall conditions. Busiest month. Book early.
  • May (early): Still good. Climbing season atmosphere. Afternoon clouds building.
  • June–August: Monsoon. Not recommended.
  • September: Transitional. Some rain early in the month, clearing by end.
  • October: Autumn peak. Exceptional clarity. Very busy.
  • November: Cold but quiet. Outstanding views. Great value.
  • December: Cold, possible snow closures. Peaceful if you're prepared.

My Recommendation

If this is your first EBC trek, go in October. The conditions are the most forgiving, the visibility is at its best, and the teahouse infrastructure is operating at full capacity. If you want fewer crowds and don't mind cold, go in November. If rhododendron forests and the energy of climbing season appeal to you, go in April.

Whatever you choose, book at least 8 weeks in advance for peak months, and make sure your travel insurance covers helicopter evacuation. The mountain doesn't care about your calendar.
