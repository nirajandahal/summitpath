Overview

The Everest Base Camp trek is one of the world's great adventures — a 130km journey through the heart of the Khumbu, Nepal's most celebrated trekking region. You'll walk in the footsteps of legendary mountaineers like Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay, passing through the bustling market town of Namche Bazaar, the sacred monastery of Tengboche, and the high-altitude settlement of Gorak Shep before reaching Base Camp at 5,364m.

The trail rewards trekkers not just with altitude but with depth — rich Sherpa culture, yak caravans on ancient trade routes, prayer flags snapping in the wind, and panoramic views of Lhotse, Nuptse, Ama Dablam, and of course, Everest herself. This is not simply a walk to a destination. It is a complete immersion in one of the most awe-inspiring landscapes on Earth.

Trek Highlights

  • Stand at Everest Base Camp (5,364m) and look up at the Khumbu Icefall
  • Sunrise from Kala Patthar (5,545m) — the best viewpoint for Everest's summit
  • Visit Tengboche Monastery, the spiritual heart of the Khumbu
  • Explore Namche Bazaar, the lively Sherpa capital of the Himalayas
  • Cross iconic suspension bridges strung with prayer flags over deep gorges
  • Experience genuine Sherpa hospitality in family-run teahouses

Day by Day

Itinerary

D1
Kathmandu — Arrival & Briefing
Arrive in Kathmandu. Transfer to hotel, trek briefing with your lead guide, gear check, and welcome dinner. Overnight at 1,400m.
D2
Fly Kathmandu → Lukla, Trek to Phakding
35-minute mountain flight to Lukla (2,840m), then a 3–4 hour walk down through rhododendron forest to Phakding (2,610m).
D3
Phakding → Namche Bazaar
Follow the Dudh Koshi river valley, cross iconic suspension bridges, and climb steeply to the bustling Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar (3,440m). First glimpse of Everest en route.
D4
Acclimatisation Day — Namche Bazaar
Rest and acclimatise. Optional hike to Everest View Hotel (3,880m) for panoramic views of Everest, Lhotse and Ama Dablam. Explore Namche market.
D5
Namche → Tengboche
Trail through pine and juniper forest with constant Himalayan views. Arrive at Tengboche (3,860m) — home to the valley's most famous monastery. Attend evening prayers if timing allows.
D6
Tengboche → Dingboche
Descend to the Imja Khola valley, then climb through increasingly sparse vegetation to Dingboche (4,410m). Dramatic views of Lhotse's south face.
D7
Acclimatisation Day — Dingboche
Hike to Nagarjun Hill (5,100m) for views of Makalu, Baruntse, and the Island Peak. Afternoon rest and hydration.
D8
Dingboche → Lobuche
Trek along the lateral moraine of the Khumbu Glacier. Pass the Thukla memorial chortens — tributes to climbers lost on Everest. Arrive Lobuche (4,940m).
D9
Lobuche → Gorak Shep → Everest Base Camp
The big day. Trek across the glacial moraine to Gorak Shep (5,164m), drop your bags, and continue to Everest Base Camp (5,364m). Soak in the moment. Return to Gorak Shep for the night.
D10
Kala Patthar Sunrise → Pheriche
Pre-dawn climb to Kala Patthar (5,545m) for the finest view of Everest anywhere. Watch the summit turn gold at sunrise. Descend all the way to Pheriche (4,371m).
D11
Pheriche → Namche Bazaar
A long but rewarding descent through increasingly green terrain. Legs are tired but spirits are high. Celebrate in Namche with a hot shower and good food.
D12
Namche → Lukla
Final day on trail. Descend the Dudh Koshi valley one last time, back to Lukla (2,840m). Farewell dinner with your guide and porter team.
D13
Fly Lukla → Kathmandu
Morning flight back to Kathmandu (weather permitting). Free afternoon to rest or explore Thamel. Celebration dinner in the evening.
D14
Kathmandu — Departure
Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport for your onward flight. Trek complete.
